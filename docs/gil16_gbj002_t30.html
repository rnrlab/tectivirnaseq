GIL16 vs GBJ002 contrast

Author

Eduardo Lozano

Published

May 22, 2026

To analyze differential expression across strains and time points, we performed all statistical modeling within a single DESeq2 dataset rather than creating separate DESeq2 objects for each time point. Because the experimental design includes strong temporal dynamics associated with phage induction, library composition changes substantially across conditions. Analyzing all samples together allows DESeq2 to estimate size factors and dispersion parameters using the full dataset, thereby borrowing statistical strength across conditions and ensuring a consistent normalization framework. Importantly, replicate effects (treated as a batch factor in the model) are captured globally; splitting the dataset by time would force DESeq2 to re‑estimate normalization and dispersion independently for each subset, disrupting the shared technical structure of the experiment and potentially introducing artificial differences. Using a unified DESeq2 object therefore provides a coherent, statistically robust foundation that preserves biological comparability, controls technical variation and enables accurate strain‑level contrasts at each time point.

In order to perform the GIL16 vs GBJ002 contrast at 30 minutes, we established a log₂ fold-change (LFC) of ±0.585. This value reflects an increase or decrease in expression of 50%, depending on whether it is positive or negative, respectively. The default value is 0, but when performing the other contrasts we obtained too many differentially expressed (DE) genes. This violates one of DESeq2’s key assumptions, which is that most genes are not differentially expressed.

Code 
contrast <- results(dds, contrast = list(c("Strain_GIL16_vs_GBJ002", "Time30.StrainGIL16")), lfcThreshold = log2(1.5))
contrast_df <- as.data.frame(contrast)

contrast_df <- contrast_df %>%
  { x <- .; x$Gene_ID <- rownames(x); x } %>%
  left_join(
    annotation %>%
      filter(Gene_ID %in% rownames(contrast_df)) %>%
      dplyr::select(Gene_ID, Gene_symbol, Description, COG_category, Association, Type, Subtype, Source),
    by = "Gene_ID"
  ) %>%
  rename(
    Mean_normalized_counts = baseMean,
    LFC = log2FoldChange,
    Adjusted_p_value = padj
  ) %>%
  dplyr::select(
    Gene_ID,
    Gene_symbol,
    Description,
    COG_category,
    Association,
    Type,
    Subtype,
    Source,
    Mean_normalized_counts,
    LFC,
    Adjusted_p_value
  )

As can be seen in the summary() results, 85 upregulated and 150 downregulated genes were obtained. In addition, 291 genes were discarded due to low counts, likely because they are expressed in other contrasts. Below is an interactive table where all DE genes can be viewed individually.

MA plot

A MA plot visualizes the relationship between the mean expression and the LFC for each gene. It helps to identify genes that are differentially expressed across conditions, highlighting those with large fold changes relative to their expression level.

Code 
plotMA(contrast, alpha = 0.05, xlab = "Mean of normalized counts", ylab = "LFC")

DE genes

DE genes are defined as those with an adjusted p-value below 0.05. Among them, upregulated genes are extracted as those with a LFC > 0.58, while downregulated genes are defined as those with an LFC < -0.58.

Code 
up_genes <- rownames(subset(contrast, padj < 0.05 & log2FoldChange > log2(1.5) & !is.na(padj)))
down_genes <- rownames(subset(contrast, padj < 0.05 & log2FoldChange < -log2(1.5) & !is.na(padj)))

Volcano plot

A volcano plot displays each gene according to its LFC on the x-axis and the statistical significance (−log₁₀ adjusted p-value) on the y-axis. This time, we found many genes both upregulated and downregulated. Among the former, GBJ002-DNT_206 stands out with a LFC ≈ 3 and we found many tRNA and ribosomal proteins genes. Among the latter, a portion of the DE genes on the chromosome correspond to the prophage region. Additionally, many pBtic235 genes are downregulated, indicating that GIL16 also influences the expression of this phage-plasmid, similar to what is observed with GIL01 in the GIL01 vs GBJ002 contrast at 30 minutes and in the study by Pavlin et al. (2022).

Code 
volcano_plot(contrast_df, limits = c(-5, 5))

Enrichment analysis

In this section, we present the results of enrichment analyses based on the COG (Clusters of Orthologous Groups), KEGG (Kyoto Encyclopedia of Genes and Genomes), GO (Gene Ontology) and EC (Enzyme Commission) categories for both upregulated and downregulated genes.

COG

According to COG categories, we found positive enrichment in translation, ribosomal structure and biogenesis (J) due to 34 DE genes and negative enrichment in inorganic ion transport and metabolism (P) due to 11 DE genes.

KEGG

According to KEGG categories, we found two positive enrichments and one negative. The positive enrichments were related to translation and metabolism of cofactors and vitamins pathways due to 30 and 5 DE genes, respectively. The negative enrichment was associated with aromatic amino acid biosynthesis through anthranilate synthase component I (GBJ002_04007), anthranilate synthase (GBJ002_04008) and phosphoribosylanthranilate isomerase (GBJ002_04011) genes.

GO

The Gene Ontology (GO) is organized into three categories: Biological Process (BP), which describes the biological objectives to which gene products contribute; Molecular Function (MF), which refers to the elemental activities of gene products at the molecular level; and Cellular Component (CC), which specifies the locations within the cell where gene products are active.

  • According to BP, we found positive enrichment in processes related to translation, gene expression and proteins and proteins, macromolecules and ketones metabolic processes. Regarding negative enrichments, they are based on ions transport through heme ABC transporter substrate-binding protein IsdE (GBJ002_01760) and heme uptake proteins IsdC (GBJ002_01761 and GBJ002_01763) genes.

  • Regarding MF, we only observed positive enrichments in functions related to the binding of RNA-associated molecules.

  • Regarding CC, only positive enrichments were found, mainly related to the cytosol, ribosomes and protein complexes.

This table contains a summary of the enrichments for each category. The subcategory was used for KEGG and the description for GO. Although you can scroll, I recommend exporting using the Excel button and viewing the table in full screen.

Code
